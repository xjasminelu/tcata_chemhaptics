Please make sure your setup is ready by following these step-by-step instructions.


1. Insert 2 pipettes into silicone patch.

Press each pipette so that its tip goes through the marked orange hole. The end of the tip of the pipette should just barely reach to the open channels.

2. Place silicone patch with pipettes inserted on inner forearm.

The patch should rest on a relatively flat area of the forearm - ideally in the middle between the wrist and elbow.

3. Use bandage cloth to wrap silicone patch tightly against forearm. (Important!)

Make sure that the bandage wraps over the width of the silicone patch and that the silicone patch lies flat against your skin. This is important for ensuring less leakage and that a seal can be formed. This is more easily done if you have someone else to help you.

4. Prepare to squeeze pipettes and tap silicone patch.

On the next screen, we will be asking you to slowly dispel the liquid into the open channels. You will do this by alternating the pipette you compress. First, compress the pipette without liquid in it. Then you will alternate between the two. You can think of it as generating a continuous flow. Additionally, in between squeezing pipettes, tap your silicone patch lightly to note any sensation changes.

5. Prepare to check/uncheck sensations as you feel them.

On the next screen, we will start the timer and ask you to verbalize sensations so that we can check and uncheck sensations as you feel them. Meanwhile, you will hear a ticking noise to suggest when you should compress pipettes and tap the patch.

Please check and uncheck the sensations listed below as you feel them.

You can also use your keyboard to check/uncheck boxes of sensations.
(Use key q for 10 and w for 11).

Please follow these steps and proceed to next phase.


1. Carefully remove silicon patch.

Unwrap bandages and slide off the patch and rest to the side.

2. Wipe off any excess liquid.

Use a dry napkin to wipe off any excess liquid left behind.

Congrats! You've finished the trial.
